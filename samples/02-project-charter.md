> This charter formally authorizes the project and the project manager. It is **not** a contract.

## Project purpose

Reduce call-center load and improve customer satisfaction by giving customers a 24/7 web portal for routine requests (order status, shipment tracking, returns, and account/password self-service).

## Objectives & success criteria

- Cut routine call-center contacts by **35%** within 3 months of launch.
- Have **60%** of returns initiated online within 3 months.
- Raise CSAT for routine interactions from **71% to 81%**.
- **Launch by June 30, 2026**, within the $180,000 budget.

## High-level scope

**In scope:** order history & status, shipment tracking, online returns, account and password self-service.
**Out of scope:** native mobile app, live chat, loyalty program, changing stored payment methods.

## Summary budget

High-level estimate: **$180,000** one-time build; ~$18,000/year ongoing.

## Key stakeholders

Sponsor (Maria Alvarez), Customer Experience team, Call Center Operations, IT (David Cho, IT Director), end customers, payment/returns vendor.

## Overall project risk

**Medium** — main exposures are vendor API integration timing and protection of customer PII.

## Project manager authority

Priya Shah is authorized to manage the schedule and team and to spend up to **$180,000**. Spend above that, or changes to scope/budget/timeline, require Change Control Board approval.

## Assumptions & constraints

- **Assumptions:** the existing order system's API is available; current cloud hosting contract can be reused.
- **Constraints:** hard launch date of June 30, 2026; fixed budget of $180,000.

## Exit criteria

The project may be cancelled if the vendor API cannot be integrated by April 30, 2026, or if projected costs exceed $220,000.
