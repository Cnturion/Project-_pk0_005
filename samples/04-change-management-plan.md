## Purpose

Define how changes to scope, schedule, or budget are requested, evaluated, and approved — so the project avoids **scope creep** and every change is traceable.

## What counts as a change

Any request that alters an approved baseline: a new or modified feature, a schedule shift, or a budget change. Bug fixes that restore agreed functionality are handled as defects, not changes.

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Requestor** | Anyone on the project; submits the change request in writing with a justification. |
| **Project Manager** (Priya Shah) | Logs the request, runs the impact assessment, documents a recommendation. |
| **Change Control Board (CCB)** | Maria Alvarez (Sponsor), Priya Shah (PM), David Cho (IT Director) — approves or denies. |

## Process

1. **Submit** — requestor completes a change request (written, with reason/justification).
2. **Log** — PM records it in the [Change Log](08-change-log.xlsx).
3. **Preliminary review** — PM and subject matter experts check feasibility.
4. **Impact assessment** — effect on scope, schedule, cost, and risk.
5. **Recommendation** — PM documents a recommend/deny with rationale.
6. **CCB decision** — approve or deny. *If denied, the process stops and the status is communicated.*
7. **Update baselines** — if approved, update the plan, schedule, and scope statement.
8. **Implement & validate** the change.
9. **Communicate** the outcome to stakeholders.

## Thresholds

- Changes adding **< $5,000** and **< 3 days**: PM may approve directly, then inform the CCB.
- Anything larger, or any scope change: requires full CCB approval.

## Records

All change requests and decisions live in the [Change Log](08-change-log.xlsx) and are reviewed at the weekly status meeting.
